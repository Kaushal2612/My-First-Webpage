Shahrukh Khan



Shah Rukh Khan, born in 2 November 1965, also known as SRK, is an Indian film actor, 
producer and television personality. He is known as "Baadshah of Bollywood", "King of
 Bollywood" or "King Khan", he has appeared in more than 80 Bollywood films, and earned 
 14 Filmfare Awards. For audience size and income, he has been described as one of the 
 most successful film stars in the world. 
 
Khan started his career with appearances in several television series in the late 1980s. 
His film was Deewana in 1992. Early, Khan was recognised for portraying villainous roles 
in the films Darr (1993), Baazigar (1993) and Anjaam (1994). His  romantic films are 
Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995), Dil To Pagal Hai (1997), Kuch Kuch Hota Hai (1998) 
and Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham... (2001). He earned critical acclaim for his portrayal of 
an alcoholic in Devdas (2002), a NASA scientist in Swades (2004), a hockey coach in Chak 
De! India (2007) and a man with Asperger syndrome in My Name Is Khan (2010). The Govt. 
of India honoured him with the Padma Shri, and the Govt. of France awarded him both the 
Ordre des Arts et des Lettres and the Légion d'honneur.

Khan is the co-owner of the Indian Premier League cricket team Kolkata Knight Riders. 
Currently, he is the richest bollywood actor.
